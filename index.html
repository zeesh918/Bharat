Common Questions

Clarifying the legal landscape for our clients.

Can I get Anticipatory Bail?

Anticipatory bail is a pre-arrest legal remedy. It is granted based on the specific merits of the FIR and the apprehension of arrest. We analyze the FIR to advise if this is the right step.

What if a Cheque Bounces?

Under Section 138 of the NI Act, a legal notice must be sent within 30 days. Failure to pay allows the complainant to file a criminal case. Time is of the essence in these matters.

Do I need to attend every hearing?

In criminal cases, the accused generally must be present. However, we can file for exemption (Section 205/317 CrPC) in valid circumstances to save your time.

How long does a Divorce take?

A mutual consent divorce can take 6-18 months. Contested divorces vary significantly. We strive to expedite the process through mediation and swift procedural filings.
